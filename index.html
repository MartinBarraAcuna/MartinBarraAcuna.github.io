Philosophy & Economics

About me

My main interesest is philosophy of economics, particularly from the perspective of the philosophy of science, as well as the relationship between economics, political philosophy, and moral philosophy. My undergraduate thesis focuses on the concept of welfare in microeconomic theory, exploring the role of non-epistemic values in science and the connection between scientific theories and political decision-making. Works

Currently, I am working on the creation and translation of accessible philosophical resources in Spanish. This includes the “Formal Toolbox for Philosophy Students”, a compilation of formal tools for philosophy students; “Para todo x: Santiago”, a translation of the formal logic textbook “Forall x: Calgary”; and “Biografías de lógicos y lógicas”, the translation of the Open Logic Project's appendix of biographies. In addition, I am collaborating on an article on ontic structural realism and constraint realism in microeconomics with Carlos Romero, and developing “An Open Introduction to Game Theory for Philosophy Students”, an introductory text to game theory focused on its relevance for philosophy, with the aim of facilitating the understanding of problems of coordination, cooperation and morality.
