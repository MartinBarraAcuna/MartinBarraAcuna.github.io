LaTeX for Philosophy

Martin Barra-Acuña

What Is LaTeX?

LaTeX is a text composition system designed for writing scientific and technical documents. Unlike conventional word processors, LaTeX uses a code-based system that allows a high level of control over the structure of the document, which is especially useful for including mathematical equations, graphs and bibliographic references in a precise manner.

Why i should learn LaTeX?

Its usefulness lies in its ability to generate professional documents, and it is especially useful for areas of philosophy where many formulas and equations are involved, such as philosophy of mathematics, philosophy of physics, analytic metaphysics, formal epistemology and philosophy of logic. Its usefulness relie on its capacity to generate custimizable and suitable for work with many mathematical symbols, equations and its ease of working with cross-references

Some areas in which it is convenient to learn LaTeX

  • Logic
  • Metaphysics and ontology
  • Decision theory
  • Formal epistemology
  • Formal political philosophy
  • (Philosophy of) Cognitive Science
  • Philosophy of physics
  • Philosophy of language
  • General philosophy of science
  • Philosophy of mathematics
  • And any area of philosophy that requires a heavy use of formal elements.
