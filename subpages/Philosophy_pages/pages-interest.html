Philosophy Resources

In this section, I recommend books, articles, online resources, events, and also publish workshops in spanish that may be enriching.

Associations and Events

  • Jornadas Rolando Chuaqui: Annual congress dedicated to the philosophy of science in Chile. You can find some records on their YouTube channel.
  • Chilean Society of Philosophy of Sciences (SOCHIFIC): An organization that promotes academic and social dialogue in the philosophy of sciences in Chile, integrating and representing professionals in the area. It seeks to strengthen and disseminate the philosophy of science, fostering its impact on the generation of knowledge, social practices, and public policies. It also encourages interdisciplinary research and the institutionalization of the philosophy of sciences in the country.
  • Santiago Mind and Cognition: Research group focused on the philosophy of mind and cognitive science. Some of the topics they work on are perception, action, emotions, artificial intelligence, consciousness, and other related areas in mind and brain research.
  • SCIENTIA: Group of Studies of Philosophy of Sciences - Faculty of Philosophy and Humanities - University of Chile: Study group of Philosophy of Sciences lead by professor Cristian Soto (Click to visit their website). They carry out different instances related to the area, such as workshops, reading groups, among others.
  • Latin American Association of Analytic Philosophy: Association that brings together professionals of analytic philosophy throughout the region. Every other year, they organize the Latin American Congress of Analytic Philosophy, which could be considered the most important and largest event in the area in Spanish.
  • AFHIC – Association of Philosophy and History of Science of the Southern Cone: Association dedicated to the history and philosophy of science throughout the Southern Cone. AFHIC holds the Congress of History and Philosophy of Science of the Southern Cone.
  • SADAF: The Argentine Society of Philosophical Analysis is a non-profit civil association dedicated to promoting philosophical research in Argentina. Recognized by CONICET and the Ministry of Science and Technology of Argentina, it has trained generations of professionals under standards of excellence. Currently, it has more than 200 researchers, both trained and in training.
  • Buenos Aires Logic Group: A team of philosophers from the University of Buenos Aires and CONICET, focused on Philosophical Logic. They work at the Institute of Philosophical Research of the Argentine Society of Philosophical Analysis (IIF-SADAF). Our areas of interest include paradoxes, non-classical logics, substructural logics, model theory, proof theory, and theories of truth.
  • SEFA | Spanish Society of Analytic Philosophy: The Spanish Society of Analytic Philosophy is responsible for institutionalizing and promoting the study of analytic philosophy in Spain.
  • LogicaMX: LogicaMX is a project that aims to disseminate logic and facilitate access to high-quality material related to this area.

Online Resources

  • Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy is one of the most valuable resources for those of us dedicated to philosophy. It includes entries of the most diverse nature. From the treatment of classic authors to contemporary philosophical problems such as dialetheism, possible worlds, metaethics, ontology of mathematics, computational philosophy, among many other things that remain as work for the reader to discover!
  • PhilPapers: Online Research in Philosophy: Philpapers is a huge database of different philosophy papers in most relevant areas. Many of these papers are categorized according to the area they deal with.
  • SEFA Philosophy Encyclopedia | SEFA: This encyclopedia is a recent project. And it is edited by the Spanish Society of Analytic Philosophy (SEFA). Among its objectives is to publish entries accessible to an audience with some training or curiosity that serve as an introduction to topics that are worked on in the Society.
  • Open Logic Project Builds: The Open Logic Project is a set of educational resources on mathematical logic designed for a non-mathematically specialized audience, and is intended to be used in advanced logic courses, such as those taught in many philosophy departments. It is open source: you can download the code in LaTeX, you are free to modify it in any way and share your changes. In addition, it is collaborative: a team of people works on it using the GitHub platform, and contributions and comments are accepted. Some of the books they have at the moment are:
    • Sets, Logic, Computation: An Open Introduction to Metalogic
    • Incompleteness and Computability: An Open Introduction to Gödel's Theorems
    • Boxes and Diamonds: An Open Introduction to Modal Logic
    • Set Theory: An Open Introduction Set Theory
    • Intermediate Logic
    • What If?: An Open Introduction to Non-Classical Logics
  • forall x: Calgary. An Introduction to Formal Logic: forall x: Calgary is a comprehensive textbook on formal logic. It covers key concepts such as the validity of arguments and logical consequence, the syntax of truth-functional propositional logic (TFL) and its semantics through truth tables, as well as the syntax of first-order logic (FOL) with identity, including first-order interpretations. It also teaches how to symbolize statements in English using TFL and FOL, and presents natural deduction systems in the Fitch style for both logics. In addition, it addresses advanced topics such as modal logic, functional completeness, and soundness. It includes exercises with solutions and is available in PDF format (for on-screen reading, printing, and a special version for dyslexics), HTML (with additional accessibility features), and source code in LaTeX. For those who wish to use it in teaching, the open-source system Carnap is recommended, which is compatible with the notation and proof systems of forall x: Calgary. You can explore sample exercises from the book at carnap.io. (There is also an obsolete and unsupported proof editor/checker for the proof system used, available at proofs.openlogicproject.org.) The book has been translated into German (forall x: Dortmund) and Portuguese (Para Todxs: Natal).

Some Journals

  • Culturas Científicas: Culturas Científicas is a journal maintained by the University of Santiago de Chile, published biannually and open access.
  • THEORIA. An International Journal for Theory, History and Foundations of Science: Theoria is an open-access journal, whose issues are published every four months.
  • Colombian Journal of Philosophy of Science: The Colombian Journal of Philosophy of Science disseminates research in philosophy of science and related areas such as epistemology, logic, and cognitive sciences. It is a semi-annual publication.
  • Principia: an international journal of epistemology: Principia is an international journal of epistemology, founded in 1997, which publishes articles, discussions, and reviews on the theory of knowledge and related areas with an analytical approach. Since 2024, it is published exclusively online with four annual issues.
  • TEOREMA: A journal specializing in logic, philosophy of language, philosophical logic, philosophy of mind, philosophy and history of science, theory of knowledge, and other related areas.
